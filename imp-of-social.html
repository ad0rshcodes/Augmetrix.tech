Importance of social media in a teenager’s growth

  • Prarthana and Disha
  • Oct 11,2021
LinkedIn

Other than our classic social networking applications, many teens nowadays are turning towards a more professional platform, LinkedIn. In more than one way, LinkedIn actually proves to be helpful in shaping a teen’s career. In today’s competitive world, getting into a college is harder than ever. Being on LinkedIn helps students look for jobs or internships which will help in building their college profile. It also helps them connect with people who can further guide them on their journey and also with like minded teens who are themselves on the road of building a better future. Having a good LinkedIn profile is also a great way of “marketing” yourself to employers or admission officers. On this platform, one may also stumble upon several opportunities, which may help one in not only boosting their profile but to gain experience in various fields of knowledge. In conclusion, linkedin has helped teenagers grow professionally and is an overall good platform for networking.

Instagram

Often seen as a negative influence by adults, Instagram is a big convenience for everyone if used in the right way. According to a survey conducted in 2021, 81% of Indian teens ranked Instagram as their favourite social media app. The present generation doesn’t lack talented people and hence everybody’s always in the search of a platform to showcase their talent. Instagram provides a free and ideal platform for teens to interact with the worldwide public. Moreover, one has the advantage of conducting live sessions with one’s followers. One can even make money by grabbing the attention of various sponsors. Lastly, gradually a lot of small businesses are shifting online to attract a larger audience hence Instagram has become even a shopping hub.

Snapchat

This social networking app owns the heart of almost every teenager. It is a fairly creative app which can lift people’s mood with its quirky filters. Moreover, Snapchat focuses on chatting with friends rather than merely posting status updates, like some of the other apps, allowing teens to maintain a bond. On the darker side, this app has led to body shaming in many individuals. ​​Snapchat filters manipulate the image of the user’s face or body shape, which can result in self-shaming and confidence dip once the filter disappears. In conclusion it’s a fairly good and secure app when it comes to clicking pictures and building a network of friends, but it can also give rise to self hatred and further negativity.

Twitter

Twitter is more likely to be used by the older teens rather than the younger ones. Unlike other social networking sites (like Instagram and Snapchat) where one usually communicates with only those one wants to, Twitter enables a person to have an open conversation with anyone and everyone. It has become a source of information for many. Even a lot of influential leaders have taken to Twitter. Also, in this era of aware citizens, it has become a popular platform to address one’s unheard issues. A tweet made publicly contributes to a lot of pressure on the party against whom the complaint has been tweeted.

We don't have a choice on whether we do social media, the question is how well we do it.
