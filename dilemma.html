The Dilemma Of Choosing

  • Arnav Patil
  • Oct 5, 2021
What do I want to do in life?

We all have asked ourselves this question at some point and if you haven’t, then you will have to someday. For many of us, this day arises somewhere near the completion of 10th standard. Everyone around you starts asking which stream you are going to choose and if you haven’t decided your stream yet, there’s a high chance that you might answer that you are opting for the same stream as the majority. As the quote goes, “Only dead fish go with the flow.”

I was completely baffled when I was asked this question. The reason not being I didn’t want to do engineering but the reason being I did want to do it, ever since childhood. From the time I watched all those sci-fi movies, I knew exactly what I wanted to do in life. So why was I confused now? Why was I questioning my decision? I am sure that this happens with many high schoolers and that many of the readers will even be able to relate to me. Now that I look back, I realise that I used to live in a bubble; I think we all do to some extent. What happens, as a child, is that we think, for example, if we become engineers we would be able to build robots and all that cool stuff that they show on those fictional shows and movies (no offence to robotic engineers!). However, as we start looking for ‘career options and opportunities’, that bubble suddenly pops and we realise that this is not how the real world works. So now you start asking yourself, “What do I want to become?” and you start making a list of your interests just to realise that the list never ends. There seem to be so many things that excite you but you’ve got to choose one thing. As for those who don’t seem to be figuring out even three interests to pen down, start exploring and doing things/activities that you’ve never wanted to do or just never have tried. Doing so will slowly and gradually make you incline towards a specific field i.e. you’ll start getting attracted towards things that actually interest you. Most important of all, never believe in the widespread ideology that science is for toppers, commerce is for average students and humanities/arts is for below average students.

If you’re still confused, what should be done?

It is also to be noted that the time has gone when people used to make wrong decisions regarding their profession and regret it all their life. Hence, whatever choice you make, it isn’t going to be the last one. Even though it is very important to make this choice, it can always be rethought upon.

When you are between the ages of 15–17, taking a decision counts, more than taking the ‘right’ one. Actually, there is no such thing as right or wrong, you never know which path might lead you where. Have faith in the decision you take and give it your best shot. At this age you haven’t explored enough to know what suits you 100%. Know that if you have even an idea about what you want to pursue, you’ve already accomplished a lot! Hope my experiences could give you some insight .


A career is not just about earning an income, it is about pursuing the essence of your life.

Arnav Patil
