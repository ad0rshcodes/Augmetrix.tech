Hacking the human brain

  • Arnav Patil
  • Sept 12, 2021

You might ask, “How is hacking the human brain possible?”. Well, this blog exists because human brain hacking exists!

There are approximately 86 billion neurons in the human brain and these 86 billion neurons use electrical signals to communicate with each other. Our brain is like an electrical device and therefore we can connect it to the working of an electronic device. A technology called Electroencephalography (EEG) already exists and is used to monitor brain activity by doctors.

But this just doesn’t seem to satisfy us; now we want our brain waves to do some real work. Excess of advancement in neuroscience and engineering to transform science fiction into reality might be one reason why we are on the verge of a catastrophe. Brain-Computer Interface (BCI), sometimes known as Mind–Machine interface (MMI), is a direct communication pathway between the brain and an external device. This collaboration of the brain and an external electronic device enables the brain signals to carry out an external activity, like moving a prosthetic hand or moving a cursor.

Now that we know some basics about how we can eventually become cyborgs, let’s see what companies are working on to develop effective brain-machine interfaces for various purposes.

Neuralink

Founded by Elon Musk, Neuralink endeavours to create a superfast, high bandwidth brain-machine interface that can help the brain work better using artificial intelligence. Neuralink is also working on designing the first neural implant, by the name of N1 Link, that will allow the brain to control a computer or a mobile from anywhere.

Neurable

Neurable describes itself as a company that translates brain activity into simple, actionable insights that we can use in our everyday life. The company made news in 2017 for inventing the world’s first brain-controlled virtual reality (VR) game. Recently, they revealed a brain-control interface enabled headphones called Enten.



Emotiv, Kernel, Next Mind, MELTIN MMI are some other companies that work in the field of brain-computer interface technology.

All this stuff is cool right? However, everything comes at a price and the price that we’ll have to pay for such technology is data. Not just any random data, but the information stored in our brain,the most private of all information. This digitally stored neural data could be used inappropriately by companies. Since neuroscience is an emerging field and nobody knows how it will unfold, we need to make the right use of this technology while also being cautious.

These are a few of the many disruptive adventures in the STEM domain. At Augmetrix.tech, we are determined to keep you updated with all recent researches and expeditions through our blogs.
